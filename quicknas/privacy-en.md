# QuickNAS Privacy Policy

Last updated: February 13, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how QuickNAS ("we", "us", "our") processes information when you use the QuickNAS app on macOS.

## 1. Scope

This Policy applies to QuickNAS app usage and support interactions via `support@unko.fun`.

It does not cover third-party services (for example, Synology services, Apple services, or your email provider), which are governed by their own policies.

## 2. Data We Process

QuickNAS is designed to operate primarily on your device. Depending on features you use, the App may process:

### A. Account and connection settings

- Display name
- Username
- Connection method (Direct URL or QuickConnect)
- Endpoint address / QuickConnect ID
- Connection security preference (for example, allowing insecure connection)

### B. Session credentials

- Session identifiers/tokens returned by NAS login APIs
- Stored in macOS Keychain when session persistence is enabled by app behavior

### C. Transfer and settings data

- Transfer history metadata (file name, status, timestamps, progress, optional share link/error message)
- App settings (upload path rules, duplicate-file handling, language/appearance, notification preferences, package size limit, onboarding completion state)

### D. Local diagnostics

- Recent client logs (connection/session/transfer related)
- App performance/cache indicators shown in Settings

### E. Optional support package (only when you choose to report a bug)

When you trigger "Report Bug", QuickNAS may prepare an email draft and attach diagnostic files for your review, such as:

- App version/build and bundle identifier
- macOS version and locale
- Language setting
- Active account display name and sanitized endpoint summary
- Connection state
- Recent client logs

You decide whether to send the email and attachments.

## 3. What We Do Not Intentionally Collect as a Cloud Service

QuickNAS does not provide a mandatory QuickNAS cloud account service for core app use.

We do not run built-in ad profiling or behavioral advertising in the app.

## 4. How We Use Data

We process data to:

- Connect and authenticate with your NAS
- Upload files and execute transfer workflows
- Apply your preferences and route files to configured paths
- Show transfer state, connection state, and storage overview
- Troubleshoot issues when you request support
- Maintain app security and reliability

## 5. Data Sharing

We share/process data with third parties only as required for app functions you invoke:

- Synology/NAS endpoints you configure (including QuickConnect when used)
- Apple system services (for example Keychain, notification framework, share/email compose services)
- Your chosen email client/provider when you send support emails

We do not sell your personal data.

## 6. Data Retention

Retention is generally controlled by local app state and your actions:

- Account settings and transfer history remain until you edit/remove them
- Session credentials remain in Keychain until replaced, invalidated, or removed
- Client logs persist locally until cleared or rotated by app behavior
- You can clear logs/cache and remove accounts from the app

## 7. Security

We apply reasonable technical measures in the app context, including use of macOS Keychain for session credential storage.

No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure. You are responsible for securing your device and NAS environment.

## 8. International Processing

Because your configured NAS or support email providers may be in different regions, data may be processed in multiple jurisdictions.

## 9. Your Choices

You can:

- Stop using the app at any time
- Remove accounts and clear local logs/cache
- Decide whether to send support emails/attachments
- Configure connection behavior, including secure/insecure access options where available

## 10. Children

QuickNAS is not directed to children under the age required by applicable law in your region.

## 11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The "Last updated" date reflects the latest revision.
